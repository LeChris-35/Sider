MOOSE
A moose is the largest member of the deer family, known for its long legs, bulky body, humped shoulders, and distinctive broad, flat antlers (in males). Native to northern forests of North America, Europe, and Asia, moose are typically solitary animals and are well-adapted to cold climates. They feed on leaves, bark, and aquatic plants, and are strong swimmers. Despite their size and slow gait, moose can run fast and become aggressive if threatened.

BUCK
A buck is a male deer, typically known for its antlers, which it sheds and regrows each year. Bucks are agile, alert animals often found in forests, grasslands, and wooded areas. They use their antlers for defense and during mating season to compete for females.

ELEPHANT
An elephant is the largest land animal, known for its long trunk, large ears, and tusks. Found in Africa and Asia, elephants are intelligent, social, and live in herds. They use their trunks for breathing, feeding, and communication.

JAGUAR
A jaguar is a large, powerful wild cat native to the Americas, especially rainforests in Central and South America. It has a yellow-orange coat with black rosettes and is known for its strength, agility, and excellent swimming ability. Jaguars are solitary and top predators.
